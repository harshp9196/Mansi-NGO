The Self-Discovered Road

According to her family members, she was illiterate, the one who doesn't know anything about the world, she was undervalued, underrated, can be easily fooled.

Straight from the village she shifted to the city and didn’t know things are done, not even the household chores. But now she is the most efficient of them all.

Gauriben Ishwarlal Gohel joined Mansi Livelihood Services in the year 2009. Yes, she has been a part of the organization since the start. She is a perfect example of everything Mansi works for i.e., development and upliftment of the socially and economically backward women. Gauriben when joined the organization was very quiet as a person.

She couldn't speak her mind and was afraid to express her own opinions. She was suppressed by the patriarchic system. After joining to collect her first two-three salaries she came with her father-in-law. Him being the head came in to collect her salary with her. This is also an example of the deeper layers of patriarchy that run in Indian households. Gauriben may have learned a lot of things after joining Mansi Livelihood Services like cooking, Hindi Language, lightning a gas, etc., but the most important thing she learned she is as capable as the men in her house.

She can stand on her two feet and be a bread earner in the house. Her father-in-law now trusts the system of Mansi completely and Gauriben has become bold and frank while talking. After her success with Mansi Livelihood Services, she also opened a savings account and started saving money. Gauriben can now take household decisions on her own and there are no questions asked to her as the men know now that her decisions are right and are for the best of everyone's interest.

The Journey from Fearful to Fearless

This is the journey of Jyotiben Shoheliya, who has worked for Mansi Seva Sakhi Mandal for about 6 years. It hasn’t been easy for her to open up about her past before joining Mansi. Her journey is the one with an alcoholic husband and a mentally abusive household. Here is her journey where she grew to become a fearless woman.

  • Had problems in speaking up
  • Wasn’t able to go out more, didn’t know how to travel alone.
  • Had money problems at home.
  • Used to be quite and tolerated all the mental abuses thrown at her

Moreover, her condition may not have changed much but she has learned to deal with them in a better way rather than to stay quiet about it.
